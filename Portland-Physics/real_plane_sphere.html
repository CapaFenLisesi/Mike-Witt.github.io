On the left is the (2D real part) of the mathematical model. On the right is the 3D physical spin vector (sometimes called a "Bloch sphere).

  • The moving green vector in the sphere is the physical spin vector that corresponds to the moving state vector in the mathematical space on the left.
  • Note that for any spin state $|\psi\rangle$ the state vector $-|\psi\rangle$ will correspond to the the same physical spin vector as $|\psi\rangle$
  • Therefore, the the physical vector goes all the way around twice, for every one time the state vector goes around.
