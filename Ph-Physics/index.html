My main personal interest is in the interpretation (what the theories are telling us about "reality") of quantum theory and relativistic physics. However, I have no intention of trying to restrict discussion to my personal favorites. I will however, outline some of these questions, as best I understand them.

Questions

The ontological status the quantum state

Does the quantum state (sometimes called the "wave function") represent something in the "real" world? Or is it just a calculational tool or a "measure of our ignorance" about ... something? Another way to ask this question would be: What is a superposition really? Note that every state is a superpostion in some basis.

The measurement problem

(Non-relativistic) quantum mechanics provides a continuous, deterministic formula for the evolution of a quantum state, via the Schroedinger equation. Until a "measurement" happens. Then everything changes. But there is no general agreement on when or how a measurement occurs. In fact many people suspect that a "state changing" measurement is a fiction. Many of the various "interpretations" of QM are the result of trying to solve this problem in various ways.

Locality

Did John Bell "prove" that quantum physics is non-local, therefore the world is non-local. Or did he rather show that if we try to replace quantum theory with a classical theory, then that classical theory must be non-local? Does this just turn out to be a disagreement about the definition of "locality" or is there a deeper debate happening?
